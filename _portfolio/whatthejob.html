UX/UI Design

What the Job? A Semi-Serious Way to Find out Your Job Match

▷ View Prototype

Website (2021)

Duration: 1 month / Designed with Figma

*Academic project created in collaboration with Cátia Neto, Gonçalo Pascoa and Teresa Fernandes (2021).

Problem

Looking at the job landscape in digital design, it can be confusing. The number of different titles, skills and responsibilities, often overlapping, can leave you wondering “Where exactly do I fit in?”


While we will not attempt to completely solve this issue, we thought that maybe we could help with the resulting identity crisis in a fun way.

Process

Discover

First Research

We started by searching for jobs opportunities to learn what titles and skills associated were out there.

We came to the conclusion that it is a complex landscape. So early on we thought of defining major areas against which we could analyse traits and skills. We came up with these five areas.

Benchmark

We looked at different types questionnaires, focusing on personality traits or preferences.

Who is This For?

There are many possibilities of specific targets, yet we wanted to do something simple which could be entertaining for a wider audience.


The primary target are younger professionals who are still a bit unfamiliar with job roles and responsibilities.


But this could also be a fun experience to a more senior professional transitioning from another area (e.g.: graphic designer going into digital design).


Overall we wanted to create an entertaining moment which would in turn have some level of practical use.

Define

Looking at all the benchmark and research, we made the choice to go with a lighter approach for this project. Using the logic of personality traits and skills to help the user figure out who they are, and where they best fit.


But we wanted to try something different.

How might we get insight into someone’s traits, skills, and preferences without asking questions?

Develop

Insight

Instead of asking questions, we'll use everyday objects or tools to give us insight into the user's traits and skills. We’re creating a sort of visual quiz.


For e.g., a wacom pen tablet would most likely be used by a UI designer, while a special keyboard would most likely be used by a programmer.


Next, we selected different objects and the areas to which they match.

Depending on the number of objects of each area selected, the user would get a percentage which then would inform the area with highest score, give a brief description of traits and responsibilities to look for in a job, while also suggesting the most likely fit in terms of job title.


At this stage we also defined a set of job title results the platform would deliver.

Prioritising

There were many ideias on the table, so we used the MoSCoW method to narrow down what we were actually going to do for this project. Out of all our ideas, which ones we would go with at this stage.

The “must have” for the project was to have a questionnaire that would result in a profile that would in turn match to job titles.


This result includes a brief description and a percentage for each of the five areas we defined. Then the platform suggests the best match in terms of job title with a description of skills and responsibilities.

Wireframes

Once we had are priorities clear we moved on to testing what the platform might be organised.

As a "should have", we proposed that this platform have a simple job board integrated, thus giving it a more practical function, adding value to the experience.


The job board is merely a repository of posting that link to the job details page in their original website.

Solution

Welcome Page

This is the welcoming screen where we have a brief disclosure of what this is.

We want to make the experience more approachable, so we ask the usar to tell us their name before starting.


In the next screen we introduce the mechanics of the experience.

The Quizz

Here we see the first screen of the quiz, where the user is invited to choose objects they’d keep on their desk.


For the interaction with the platform, we wanted to use drag and drop when the user chose the objects.

They’d have to pick it up and drop it in the selection bar at the bottom of the screen.


When they're done, they move on to the next screen where the platform will give a commentary on the choices.

Phases

To keep the experience short and avoid abandonments, we organised it into three moments: physical space, digital tools, and break time. In each round the user will choose objects that better suit them in a total of 10 choices across all three rounds.

After filling all the slots, the user will get their results.

Results Page

Here we see the results page with all the features we wanted to include:


  • → brief description
  • → percentage breakdown
  • → top job title match
  • → information on skills and responsibilities of this job
  • → a couple of other job title options
  • → a few job opportunities that best match the results

The user can also share their results on social media or they can retake the quiz.
